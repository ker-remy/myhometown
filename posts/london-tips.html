Artisans Angkor: Local Crafts, Workshops, Shopping, and Visitor Tips

On this page

  • Workshops
  • Crafts & Shopping
  • Community Impact & Visitor Tips

Workshops

Artisans Angkor offers free guided tours where visitors can observe Khmer craftsmanship up close. Skilled artisans demonstrate traditional techniques such as wood carving, stone sculpting, silk painting, and silver plating. Each workshop highlights how these ancient crafts are being preserved and passed down to younger generations. It’s fascinating to watch their patience and precision as every piece slowly comes to life.

Crafts & Shopping

After exploring the workshops, take time to browse the boutique shop. You’ll find handwoven silk scarves, detailed carvings, jewelry, and home décor all made by local artisans. Prices are fair, and every purchase helps support Cambodia’s creative community. Even if you don’t plan to buy much, it’s worth walking through just to admire the artistry and design.

Community Impact & Visitor Tips

Artisans Angkor was founded to revive Cambodia’s traditional crafts and provide fair employment for young artisans. Today, it supports hundreds of families and sustains local villages through skill-based work.

Visitors can explore the workshops and boutique on Stung Thmey Street, near the Old Market area. Entry is free, with guided tours offered several times a day. It’s best to visit in the morning when the studios are most active. Photography is welcome, but please ask before taking close-up pictures. Bring light clothing and water, as the weather can be quite warm around midday.
