Travel Essentials: Cambodia

Quick, practical notes to plan a smooth Cambodian journey.

Top Spots

Angkor Wat and Siem Reap temples, Phnom Penh’s Royal Palace, riverside Kampot & Kep, laid-back Battambang, and the beaches of Koh Rong.

Local Etiquette

Dress modestly in temples, remove hats, ask before photographing monks, and greet with a friendly smile.

Currency & Costs

USD and KHR both circulate; carry small bills. ATMs are common in cities. Negotiate tuk‑tuks politely.

Connectivity

Local SIMs (Smart, Cellcard, Metfone) and eSIMs work well; data is affordable and widely available.

Transport

Tuk‑tuks are common; ride‑hailing apps vary by city. Buses connect major routes; domestic flights reach Siem Reap and Sihanoukville.

Safety & Health

Drink bottled/filtered water, use sun protection, and consider travel insurance. Emergencies: 117 Police, 118 Fire, 119 Ambulance.

Khmer Basics

Hello: សួស្តី (Suosdei), Thank you: អរគុណ (Orkun), Yes/No: បាទ/ចាស (Baht/Chaas) / ទេ (Te).

Best Seasons

Cool/dry Nov–Feb; hot Mar–May; rainy Jun–Oct with lush scenery and afternoon showers.
